# AI Literacy Training Program

**Document ID:** TRAIN-AI-001
**Effective Date:** 2025-09-01
**Owner:** HR & AI Governance Lead
**Classification:** Internal

---

## 1. Purpose

This document outlines Acme Corp's AI literacy training program, ensuring compliance with Article 4 of the EU AI Act, which requires providers and deployers to ensure sufficient AI literacy among their staff.

## 2. Training Tiers

### Tier 1: AI Awareness (All Employees)

**Duration:** 2 hours (online, self-paced)
**Frequency:** Annual
**Audience:** All Acme Corp employees

**Curriculum:**
1. What is AI and how does it differ from traditional software?
2. Types of AI systems (rule-based, ML, generative AI)
3. Overview of the EU AI Act and why it matters
4. AI risks: bias, fairness, transparency, safety
5. Acme Corp's AI Governance Policy overview
6. How to report AI-related concerns

**Assessment:** 10-question quiz, pass mark 80%

### Tier 2: AI System Users (Recruiters)

**Duration:** 6 hours (blended: 4h online + 2h workshop)
**Frequency:** Before system access + annual refresh
**Audience:** All employees who use the AI Hiring Tool

**Curriculum:**
1. How the AI Hiring Tool works (system overview, not technical details)
2. Understanding suitability scores and confidence intervals
3. Interpreting SHAP explanations
4. When and how to override AI scores
5. Recognizing signs of AI malfunction or bias
6. Candidate rights and how to respond to inquiries
7. Hands-on practice with the recruiter dashboard
8. Scenario-based exercises (bias detection, override decisions)

**Assessment:** Practical exercise + 15-question quiz, pass mark 80%

### Tier 3: AI Developers (ML/Data Team)

**Duration:** 16 hours (workshop-based over 2 days)
**Frequency:** Semi-annual
**Audience:** ML engineers, data engineers, data scientists

**Curriculum:**
1. EU AI Act deep dive (requirements for high-risk systems)
2. Responsible AI development practices
3. Bias detection and mitigation techniques
4. Fairness metrics implementation (AIF360, demographic parity, etc.)
5. Privacy-preserving ML techniques
6. Security considerations in ML systems
7. Testing for robustness and adversarial inputs
8. Documentation requirements (model cards, technical docs)
9. Post-deployment monitoring best practices
10. Incident response for AI systems

**Assessment:** Hands-on project + peer review

### Tier 4: AI Governance & Leadership

**Duration:** 4 hours (executive briefing)
**Frequency:** Annual
**Audience:** AI Governance Board, senior management

**Curriculum:**
1. Regulatory landscape update (EU AI Act, national implementations)
2. Risk and liability implications for leadership
3. Governance structure effectiveness review
4. Industry benchmarks and best practices
5. Upcoming regulatory milestones and deadlines

**Assessment:** Discussion-based, no formal assessment

## 3. Completion Tracking

### 3.1 Requirements

| Role | Required Tiers | Deadline |
|------|---------------|----------|
| All employees | Tier 1 | Within 90 days of hire |
| Recruiters using AI tool | Tier 1 + Tier 2 | Before system access |
| ML/Data engineers | Tier 1 + Tier 3 | Before development work |
| AI Governance Board | Tier 1 + Tier 4 | Within 30 days of appointment |

### 3.2 Tracking Metrics

| Metric | Target | Current (Q4 2025) |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Tier 1 completion rate (all staff) | 100% | 87% |
| Tier 2 completion rate (recruiters) | 100% | 100% |
| Tier 3 completion rate (developers) | 100% | 95% |
| Tier 4 completion rate (leadership) | 100% | 100% |
| Average quiz score (Tier 1) | > 85% | 88% |
| Average quiz score (Tier 2) | > 85% | 91% |

### 3.3 Non-Compliance

- Employees who do not complete required training within the deadline will have system access suspended
- Repeated non-compliance escalated to line manager and HR
- Training completion is included in performance review criteria

## 4. Training Materials

All training materials are:
- Available in English and German
- Accessible per WCAG 2.1 AA standards
- Updated within 30 days of significant regulatory or system changes
- Stored in the corporate LMS with version control

## 5. Continuous Learning

Beyond formal training:
- Monthly AI governance newsletter
- Quarterly "AI Ethics Hour" discussion sessions
- Access to external resources (AI ethics courses, regulatory updates)
- Encouraged participation in industry conferences and working groups
