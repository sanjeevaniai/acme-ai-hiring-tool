## 1. Purpose

This document defines the roles, obligations, and compliance requirements for all actors in the AI Hiring Tool's supply chain, per Articles 25-28 of the EU AI Act.

## 2. Value Chain Actors

### 2.1 Provider (Acme Corp)

**Role:** Provider of the AI Hiring Tool (Article 3(3))

**Obligations:**
- Ensure the AI system complies with all requirements of Title III, Chapter 2
- Establish and maintain a quality management system (Article 17)
- Draw up technical documentation (Article 11)
- Keep logs generated by the AI system (Article 12)
- Undergo conformity assessment (Article 43)
- Affix CE marking and draw up declaration of conformity
- Register in the EU database (Article 49)
- Take corrective action when the system is not in conformity
- Cooperate with competent authorities

### 2.2 Deployers (Client Organizations)

**Role:** Deployer of the AI Hiring Tool (Article 3(4))

**Obligations per Article 26:**
- Use the system in accordance with instructions for use
- Ensure human oversight by trained personnel
- Monitor the AI system for risks during operation
- Inform the provider of any serious incidents
- Conduct a fundamental rights impact assessment (Article 27)
- Inform natural persons subject to AI system decisions

## 3. Deployer Agreements

### 3.1 Contractual Requirements

All deployer agreements include:
- Obligation to follow instructions for use
- Requirement for human oversight implementation
- Data processing agreement (GDPR Article 28)
- Incident reporting obligations (within 24 hours)
- Audit rights for Acme Corp (annual)
- Training completion requirements before system activation
- Prohibition on system modification without approval

### 3.2 Deployer Onboarding Process

1. **Compliance Review:** Deployer's readiness assessed against checklist
2. **Agreement Execution:** Service agreement + DPA signed
3. **Training:** All users complete mandatory training program
4. **Technical Integration:** System integrated with deployer's ATS
5. **Validation:** Pilot period with enhanced monitoring (30 days)
6. **Go-Live:** Full deployment with standard monitoring

## 4. Authorized Representative

Acme Corp is established in the EU (Germany) and therefore does not require an authorized representative under Article 25.

If Acme Corp operates outside the EU in the future, an authorized representative will be appointed per Article 22 requirements.

## 5. Importer/Distributor Obligations

Currently not applicable as:
- Acme Corp is the sole provider
- The system is provided directly to deployers (no distributors)
- No importation from outside the EU

If the distribution model changes, importer (Article 23) and distributor (Article 24) obligations will be documented and enforced.

## 6. Supply Chain Communication

### 6.1 Information Sharing

| From | To | Information | Frequency |
|------|-----|------------|-----------|
| Provider → Deployer | Performance reports, updates, incidents | Monthly |
| Deployer → Provider | Usage metrics, incident reports, feedback | Monthly |
| Provider → Authority | Serious incident reports | Per occurrence |
| Authority → Provider | Inspection findings, requests | Per occurrence |

### 6.2 Incident Escalation

1. Deployer detects issue → Reports to Provider within 24 hours
2. Provider assesses severity → Classifies per incident management framework
3. If serious incident → Provider reports to market surveillance authority within 15 days
4. Provider communicates resolution to all affected deployers
