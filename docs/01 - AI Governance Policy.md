# Acme Corp - AI Governance Policy

**Document ID:** GOV-AI-001
**Effective Date:** 2025-06-01
**Review Date:** 2026-06-01
**Approved By:** Chief Technology Officer, General Counsel
**Classification:** Internal

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the governance framework for the development, deployment, and operation of artificial intelligence (AI) systems at Acme Corp. It ensures compliance with the EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689), GDPR, and applicable national implementing legislation.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All AI systems developed, deployed, or procured by Acme Corp
- All employees, contractors, and third parties involved in AI activities
- All stages of the AI lifecycle: design, development, testing, deployment, monitoring, and retirement

## 3. Governance Structure

### 3.1 AI Governance Board

**Composition:** CTO (Chair), General Counsel, Chief Data Officer, CISO, Head of HR, External Ethics Advisor

**Responsibilities:**
- Approve deployment of new AI systems
- Review quarterly compliance reports
- Set risk appetite and governance priorities
- Oversee incident response for AI-related issues

**Meeting Cadence:** Monthly, with emergency sessions as needed

### 3.2 AI Governance Lead

**Reports to:** CTO

**Responsibilities:**
- Day-to-day governance operations
- Risk assessment coordination
- Compliance monitoring and reporting
- Training program management
- Regulatory engagement

### 3.3 AI Ethics Committee

**Composition:** Cross-functional representatives including employee representatives

**Responsibilities:**
- Review ethical implications of AI systems
- Assess fundamental rights impact
- Advise on fairness and bias matters
- Review complaints and appeals from affected persons

## 4. Risk Classification

### 4.1 Classification Process

All AI systems must be classified according to the EU AI Act risk tiers:

| Tier | Classification | Governance Requirements |
|------|---------------|----------------------|
| Prohibited | Article 5 practices | Must not be developed or deployed |
| High-Risk | Annex III systems | Full compliance framework required |
| Limited Risk | Transparency obligations | Disclosure requirements |
| Minimal Risk | Voluntary compliance | Best practices encouraged |

### 4.2 Current AI Systems

| System | Risk Classification | Status |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| AI Hiring Tool | High-Risk (Annex III, 4a) | Active - Full compliance |
| Customer Chatbot | Limited Risk | Active - Transparency compliance |
| Internal Analytics | Minimal Risk | Active - Best practices |

## 5. Quality Management System

### 5.1 Requirements

All high-risk AI systems must operate under a quality management system (QMS) that includes:

- Written policies and procedures for each compliance area
- Risk management process (see Risk Management Framework)
- Data governance procedures
- Technical documentation
- Record-keeping and logging
- Corrective and preventive actions
- Post-market monitoring

### 5.2 Documentation Standards

- All QMS documents follow controlled document procedures
- Version control with approval workflow
- Annual review cycle (minimum)
- Audit trail for all changes

## 6. Accountability

### 6.1 Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| AI Governance Board | Strategic oversight and approval |
| AI Governance Lead | Operational compliance management |
| Development Teams | Implement compliance requirements |
| Data Protection Officer | GDPR alignment and DPIA |
| CISO | Cybersecurity controls |
| HR | Employee AI literacy training |

### 6.2 Escalation Path

1. Development team identifies issue
2. AI Governance Lead assesses severity
3. High/Critical: Escalate to AI Governance Board within 24 hours
4. Board decides on system pause, remediation, or reporting to authorities

## 7. Regulatory Engagement

### 7.1 Registration

High-risk AI systems will be registered in the EU database per Article 49 requirements before market placement.

### 7.2 Cooperation with Authorities

Acme Corp will cooperate fully with national competent authorities and market surveillance authorities, providing:
- Access to documentation upon request
- Technical explanations of system behavior
- Incident reports within required timelines
- Corrective action plans when required

### 7.3 Regulatory Monitoring

The AI Governance Lead monitors regulatory developments including:
- EU AI Act implementing acts and delegated acts
- Harmonised standards published by CEN/CENELEC
- Guidance from the AI Office
- National implementation measures

## 8. Training and Awareness

### 8.1 Mandatory Training

| Audience | Training | Frequency |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| All employees | AI awareness basics | Annual |
| AI developers | EU AI Act compliance for developers | Semi-annual |
| AI system users | System-specific training | Per deployment + annual refresh |
| Management | AI governance overview | Annual |

### 8.2 AI Literacy

Per Article 4 of the EU AI Act, Acme Corp ensures that staff involved in AI operations have sufficient AI literacy appropriate to their role.

## 9. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:
- Immediate suspension of AI system operations
- Disciplinary action per HR policies
- Regulatory reporting as required
- Corrective action planning and implementation

## 10. Review

This policy is reviewed annually by the AI Governance Board. Ad-hoc reviews are triggered by:
- Significant regulatory changes
- Major AI incidents
- Organizational changes affecting AI governance
